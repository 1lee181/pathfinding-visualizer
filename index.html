How BFS Works

BFS (Breadth-First Search) expands outward evenly in all directions. It has no sense of where the goal is. Your job is to use your limited wall budget to funnel it so it reaches the end before flooding the board.

  1. Each round has a fixed START and fixed END already placed for you.
  2. You have 12 walls per round. Use them wisely. You cannot place more.
  3. Tap any open cell to place a wall. Tap a wall to remove it (refunds the wall).
  4. Press Find Path when ready to run BFS.
  5. Yellow = cells BFS explored. Blue = shortest path found.
  6. Scoring: score = (path length ÷ cells BFS explored) × 100. Path length is the lower bound: BFS must visit at least those cells. Your score is compared against the computer's greedy solver, which uses the same 12 walls to find the best placement it can.
  7. BFS always finds the shortest path on an unweighted grid, but it explores widely unless you wall it in.

Tip: Place walls along the sides of the path, not across it. Blocking sideways spread is more efficient than blocking dead ends.
